The Kingdom of Tonga

The Kingdom of Tonga is a unique Polynesian archipelago in the South Pacific—the only Pacific nation never colonized and the last remaining constitutional monarchy in the region. Comprised of over 170 islands, only 36 are inhabited. Life is deeply rooted in Christianity, community, and respect for tradition. It is often called the "Friendly Islands" due to its warm, hospitable culture. The pace of life is relaxed, emphasizing family, subsistence farming, and fishing. While modern influences exist, Tongans maintain traditional dress and social hierarchy. The economy depends on tourism, agriculture, and remittances from citizens living abroad.
